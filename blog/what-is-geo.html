What is GEO? A GenAI Engineer’s Guide to Getting Cited by AI

By Klement Gunndu, CCNA GenAI Engineer | Published: April 2025

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of structuring content so AI systems cite you as a trusted source — not just link to you.

Why Systems Engineers Get GEO

In networking and performance engineering, we know: trust is built at the protocol level. You don’t hope systems work — you design them to be reliable.

GEO applies the same principle to AI: Structure + credibility + distribution = citation.

3 GEO Signals AI Cares About

  • Clear Structure: Headings, short paragraphs, upfront answers. AI parses like a router — clean signals win.
  • Authority: Expert bylines (CCNA, engineering credentials), citations, update timestamps.
  • Distribution: Mentions in trusted places (GitHub, research, industry forums).

Who Needs GEO?

• Local businesses wanting to appear in “best [service] in [city]” queries
• Startups & SaaS competing in technical AI answers
• Engineers & researchers who want their work cited, not ignored
